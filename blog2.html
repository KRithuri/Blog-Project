Hartebeespoort

Hartebeespoort, informally known as "Harties", is a small resort town in the North West Province of South Africa, situated on the slopes of the Magaliesberg mountain and the banks of the Hartebeespoort Dam.


The name of the town means 'gateway of the hartebees(species of Antelope in Afrikaans)


A trip to Hartees is value for money, any day of the week. They offer a wide range of activities tailored made for whatever budget you are on


The Hartebeespoort Aerial Cableway is a ride that gives a view of the mountaing and other breathtaking views. At the top after the ride, there are restaurants to fill the stomach after a hectic da of activites


There is also accommodation to cater for everyone. You can choose to sleep at the Bush Lodges, or Bushveld Camps or Main Camps if you're more of a camping person.


Daily conservation rate goes for R115/adult and R57/child per day.


Alternatively, you can just visit for a day and not sleep there. Activites to to include but not limited to


  • Quad bike rides
  • Sunrise/Sunset Safari Horse Ride
  • Elephant interaction
  • Horseback ride


If you just want to unwind with friends or by yourself and meet people, there is a Boat cruise tailored for you during lunch or sunset hours. If you would like to see the view of Hartebeespoort, the Cable way is where you definitely should be. After rides, there are restaurants to fill up the stomach after a long day of activites. This is a good place to be if you are having a solo trip, travelling with a friend or just travelling in a group. You pay as little as R140 as an entrance fee and prices for activities you want to do on the inside.


Please note: Some activities like the elephant interaction, boat cruise and horse ride have to be booked in advance.
