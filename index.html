Kruger National Park

Kruger National Park, in northeastern South Africa, is one of Africa’s largest game reserves. Its high density of wild animals includes the Big 5: lions, leopards, rhinos, elephants and buffalos. Hundreds of other mammals make their home here, as do diverse bird species such as vultures, eagles and storks. Mountains, bush plains and tropical forests are all part of the landscape. If you want to experiience nature at it's best then this is the place to be. There is also accommodation to cater for everyone. You can choose to sleep at the Bush Lodges, or Bushveld Camps or Main Camps if you're more of a camping person. Daily conservation rate goes for R115/adult and R57/child per day.Alternatively, you can just visit for a day and not sleep there. Activites to to include but not limited to

  • Game drives
  • Guided walks
  • Birding
  • Wilderness Trails

Gates open from 05:30-18:30

Hartebeespoort,Pretoria

Hartbeespoort is a South African resort town north of Johannesburg. It’s known for the Hartbeespoort Dam and the whitewater rapids of the Crocodile River. Both are visible from the Hartbeespoort Aerial Cableway, which ascends to the top of the Magaliesberg mountains. Outside of town, Miracle Waters is a spring-water dive site in a former chrome mine. Also nearby is the Ann van Dyk Cheetah Centre, a big-cat sanctuary If you are into adventure, then this is for you! Hartebeespoort offers a whole wide range of activities like

  • Quad bike rides
  • Sunrise/Sunset Safari Horse Ride
  • Elephant interaction
  • Horseback ride

and so many more.

If you just want to unwind with friends or by yourself and meet people, there is a Boat cruise tailored for you during lunch or sunset hours. If you would like to see the view of Hartebeespoort, the Cable way is where you definitely should be. After rides, there are restaurants to fill up the stomach after a long day of activites. This is a good place to be if you are having a solo trip, travelling with a friend or just travelling in a group. You pay as little as R140 as an entrance fee and prices for activities you want to do on the inside.

Please note: Some activities like the elephant interaction, boat cruise and horse ride have to be booked in advance.
